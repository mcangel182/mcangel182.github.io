Projects

BrainPerfusion

Web based application that allows doctors and researchers to share medical data and images of pacients that have suffered a stroke. Users may upload, edit, visualize and download patient information (data and images) for further analysis. The app offers two visualization modules, one for stacks of 2D Dicom images and other for 3D Nifti volumes. It also has a search module based on Solr. The plataform development was based on an PHP-based open source platform called Midas. For the visualization modules Cornerstone and Brainbrowser libraries were integrated to the platform.

SubTitles

Mobile app developed for Android and iOS that seeks to mitigate the usual problems of translation of foreign language plays, avoiding the inconveniences of projecting the subtitles somewhere on the stage (little visibility of the last rows, some object beign in front of the projection and desynchronization as a result of the human error of the person who controls them). SubTitles allows to download and visualize the subtitles of a play using auditory context management to synchronize the subtitles with the current moment of the play. To do so we used voice recognition APIs of Android and iOS.

LocalPals

Web based application that allows tourists to discover a city with a local. The app aims to conect backpackers who wish to discover cities in a different way, with local people that are thrilled to meet foreigners from other cultures. The application was the winner of that semester's "La Muestra" and was offered mentoring for future development.The project was implemented using Ruby On Rails for backend development and Backbone.js for frontend development.

MediSalud

Web based application that allows physicians to monitor their patients automating this process with the help of mobile devices. The system provides the following services: 1) monitoring the progress of patients through control panels, 2) a system of reminders so that the patient does not forget to relay the data collection, and 3) an alert system for handling any emergency in case any of the patient's data is outside the normal bounds. In order to collect data from the patients, two mobile apps (Android and iOS) were developped. The project was developped using J2EE, JSF and JPA.
